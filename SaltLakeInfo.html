Salt Lake Temple

Synopsis

The Salt Lake Temple has become an icon of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints in a variety of ways. While not the first temple ever built, it certainly is among the most widely recognized. Original construction of this temple began nearly 200 years ago in 1853, however, the prophet Brigham Young received revelation for where this temple would be built approximately 5 years earlier.

The cornerstones of the temple were laid on April 6, 1853, a capstone ceremony was held nearly 40 years later on April 6, 1892, and President Wilford Woodruff dedicated the temple 1 year later on April 6, 1893. This date is significant for several reasons, one, it is believed that April 6 may have been the date which Jesus Christ was born on. Additionally, the organization of the church also took place on April 6.
